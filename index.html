§ 04 · Leverage Analysis
Debt Trajectory & Risk Evolution

Tracking leverage, coverage adequacy, and financial risk across the full business cycle — from JLR acquisition debt load to historic deleveraging.

Debt Waterfall & Risk Flags
Total Debt trajectory with annual risk classification
Metric FY2015FY2016FY2017FY2018FY2019 FY2020FY2021FY2022FY2023FY2024
Narrative Timeline
FY2016
Net loss of ₹8,124 Cr triggered by JLR restructuring charges and weak domestic commercial vehicle demand. D/E worsened to 2.88x.
FY2019–20 · CRISIS
D/E peaked at 5.24x — JLR's Ingenium engine investment cycle + India NBFC credit crisis. Interest coverage of 0.27x signaled near-distress.
FY2021 · COVID-19
Pandemic compounded structural JLR challenges. Three consecutive years of losses eroded equity by 67% from FY2018 peak.
FY2023–24 · TURNAROUND
JLR profit recovery (Range Rover / Defender premiumisation) + India CV/PV supercycle drove historic deleveraging. FY2024 net profit ₹31,807 Cr — all-time high.
Methodology

• D/E Ratio = Total Debt / Total Equity (book value, consolidated)

• Interest Coverage = EBIT / Interest Expense

Risk Classification Thresholds:

• ICR < 0 → Critical

• 0 ≤ ICR < 1.5 → High

• 1.5 ≤ ICR < 3.0 → Moderate

• 3.0 ≤ ICR < 5.0 → Cautious

• ICR ≥ 5.0 → Healthy / Improving

EBITDA proxy = EBIT × 1.15 where depreciation not separately available
